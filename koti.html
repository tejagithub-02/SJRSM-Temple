Terah Koti Ramanama Lekhana Yagna

The "Terah Koti Ramanama Lekhana Yagna" (13 Crore Rama Nama Writing Yagna) is a profound spiritual practice where devotees write the sacred name of Lord Rama 130 million times. This act of devotion purifies the mind, deepens faith, and invites divine blessings.

What is Rama Nama Lekhana?
Rama Nama refers to the divine name of Lord Rama, an incarnation of Lord Vishnu, celebrated for his righteousness and compassion. "Lekhana" means writing, and "Yagna" denotes a Vedic ritual of devotion. In this yagna, devotees collectively aim to write "Sri Rama" or the Rama Taraka Mantra 130 million times, a monumental undertaking symbolizing unwavering dedication.

Originating from teachings of saints like Tulsidas and Swami Ramdas, this practice is a cornerstone of Ramanama Sankirtana traditions. Writing Rama's name is believed to be more powerful than Lord Rama Himself, as per the Ramcharitmanas. This repetitive act purifies the heart, fosters discipline, and aligns the devotee with divine consciousness.

Spiritual Significance:
The practice is said to bring peace, remove obstacles, and grant spiritual liberation. The collective effort of 13 crore writings amplifies this sanctity, creating a ripple of devotion across communities.

How to Participate:
Devotees use special Ramanama Likhita Japa notebooks with grids to write "Sri Rama" or "Sri Rama, Jaya Rama, Jaya Jaya Rama" 108 or 1008 times per page. Individuals may write daily, while groups coordinate thousands of devotees in collective efforts.

Global Participation:
Ashrams and virtual platforms facilitate participation worldwide, especially during festivals like Ramanavami. In programs like Shri Kshetra Hebballi, over 500 participants completed 13 crore writings over several months.
